Storyline

In 18th century France a young painter, Marianne, is commissioned to do the wedding portrait of Héloïse without her knowing. Therefore, Marianne must observe her model by day to paint her portrait at night. Day by day, the two women become closer as they share Héloïse's last moments of freedom before the impending wedding. The two become attracted to each other as they spend time together. As the deadline for the portrait approaches, Marianne confesses to Héloïse that she is painting a protrait. Héloïse collaborates for completing the portrait and accepts her unwanted marriage. The two have not seen each other after Marianne finishes her work and leaves the island.

Characters

Marianne

As a young painter, Marianne is invited to an island off the Brittany cost, to paint Héloïse, who does not want to pose. Marianne decides to accompany her and paint her from memory in secret.

Héloïse

Héloïse is going to marry to her suitor from Milan, who she has not seen before. She refuses to pose for the portrait that is going to be presented to her suitor as a way to refuse to the marriage.

Sophie

Sophie is Héloïse's servant, who accompanies Marianne and Héloïse after Héloïse's mother leaves the island in the last days of Héloïse's freedom before the painting is completed.

The Countess

Héloïse's mother, who wants Héloïse to accept her marriage and hires Marianne after several other painters fail to complete the portrait.

Scenes from the Movie

Portrait of a Lady on Fire

Portrait of a Lady on Fire is a portrait of Héloïse that Marianne painted and kept for herself. The painting captures the moment when Marianne, Héloïse, and Sophie sit by a bonfire singing and clapping and Héloïse's dress accidentally catches fire.
